The Problem Today

Two systems. One spreadsheet.
A lot of manual matching.

SPS runs both outsourced and homegrown adjudication. Every reconciliation cycle, the finance team manually normalizes data from two formats, matches payments against submissions, and investigates every discrepancy by hand.

1

Claims Adjudication

Two systems produce claims data in two different formats. Manual normalization every cycle.

Dual-system pain
2

Rebate Submission

Aggregate claims by manufacturer, format per each manufacturer's template, submit, and log what was sent.

30-90d

Manufacturer Processing

Wait for manufacturer to process and send payment. Some send detailed remittances. Some send a lump sum with no breakdown.

3

Reconciliation — "The Pain Zone"

Manually match payment amounts to submission amounts. Investigate every discrepancy: short-pays, missing payments, rejected NDCs. Track resolution via email. Produce reports for client pass-through.

Highest time & error cost

Where Dollars Leak

Five high-risk pain points

Clustering around data normalization and payment matching — where hours burn and revenue leaks.

High Risk

Two systems, two formats

Different schemas from outsourced and homegrown adjudication require manual normalization every single cycle.

High Risk

Lump-sum payments

Some manufacturers send a single number with no line-item detail. Can't tie the payment back to specific claims.

High Risk

No discrepancy tracker

Investigations live in email threads and tribal knowledge. When someone's out, context is lost.

High Risk

Client pass-through accuracy

Revenue is at risk if rebates aren't attributed to the right pharmacy client. Manual report assembly is error-prone.

Medium Risk

No audit trail

Reconciliation decisions aren't logged in a structured way. SOC2 and external audit prep is a scramble.

Medium Risk

Migration-period gaps

As SPS transitions to homegrown-only, the same claim could appear in either system. Increases matching complexity.

The Vision

AI investigates. Humans decide.

Every AI output has a confirm, edit, or reject control. Nothing executes without human approval. The system earns trust through tracked accuracy over time.

Step 1

Unified Data Ingestion

Claims from both adjudication systems normalized into one format, one place. Source-tagged so you always know where each claim came from.

Automated

Step 2

Submission Tracking

Every rebate submission logged with manufacturer, period, amount, and expected payment date. Auto-alerts if payment is overdue.

Human logs

Step 3

AI-Assisted Payment Matching

Upload a remittance document. AI extracts structured data with source citations. Deterministic rules verify the math. You confirm with one click.

AI + Human review

Step 4

Discrepancy Management

Every variance gets a tracked record. AI identifies which NDCs account for the difference and proposes root cause. You approve or edit.

AI + Human review

Step 5

Drafted Communications

AI drafts follow-up emails with specific amounts, NDCs, and submission references. You review and send. No one writes these from scratch.

AI drafts, Human sends

Step 6

Auto-Generated Reports

Reconciliation summaries, client pass-through reports, aging dashboards, and full audit trail. Generated automatically each cycle.

Automated

Design Principles

How we'll build it

Three constraints that guide every decision.

AI is never the last line of defense

AI reads documents and proposes matches. Deterministic rules verify the math. Humans make the final call. The system earns trust through tracked accuracy — never through promises.

Survives after the internship

Built on SharePoint + Power Automate + Power BI. No-code maintenance surface. Your team owns it. No developer needed to keep it running.

Survives the system migration

The data ingestion step normalizes from any source. When you move to homegrown-only, swap the input — not the tool. The automation gets simpler, not obsolete.
